About

Due to a lack of emphasis on sustainability, millions around the world suffer from issues that could be reduced immensely through global cooperation. These issues include issues that you, as a global citizen, have heard about or may even experience ranging from poverty and economic growth to climate change and sanitation. The United Nations has led a tremendous effort to increase global cooperation to solve these issues by introducing the 17 Sustainable Development Goals. However, introducing them is simply not enough to ensure we have a brighter, better, and cleaner future. We need to act by them, and the first step to doing so is to have awareness about the goals.

Thus, that brings us to you and me, global citizens of this big, beautiful world. We can do a lot to help. Through the Urban Sustainability Game, you’ll not only learn about the several different SDGs we’ve selected but also have the chance to experience what it’s like to be a leader.

In this role, you’ll be given a village, town, or city, 2 UN Sustainable Development Goals, and 15 years to successfully develop your area. You must stay out of debt and achieve both goals to be a sustainable leader with a sustainable city. Are you up to the challenge?

Central Place Theory

This menu design was inspired by the very principles of the Central Place Theory by Walter Christaller, showing the relation of central markets to its hinterland (region of market). This theory shows the interaction between various villages (smaller hexagons), towns (medium-sized), and cities (center of medium-sized hexagons), and how they interact based off the various ranges, how far people would travel for such services.
